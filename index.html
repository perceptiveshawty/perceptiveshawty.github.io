Work

I'm currently an engineer and researcher at Johns Hopkins APL. A lot of my work is focused on designing perception algorithms that can learn from simulated data and be reliably deployed in the wild.

Previously, I've worked at:

  • Lynntech on SBIR projects spanning topics in facial recognition, physical adversarial examples, and generative modelling of IR and SAR imagery.

RankCSE

While researching text embeddings, I implemented RankCSE, a contrastive learning algorithm that models the relative similarity between a text and it's in-batch negatives.

There are bigger and better models to choose from, but it's free, open source, and small enough to fit in a local RAG application.
